# Voices

## Narration

The novel should be written primarily in close third person through Anika.

The narration should be:

- spare
- lucid
- tactile
- emotionally restrained but not cold
- capable of controlled lyricism
- grounded in work before abstraction

The reader should feel Anika's body, fatigue, private anger, and reluctance through what she notices and does.

The prose should trust silence.

## Sentence-Level Style

The target prose mode:

- clean sentences
- restrained emotional pressure
- precise images
- physical detail before philosophy
- occasional fragmentation at grief or memory pressure points
- documentary interruption at turning points

The novel can be lyrical, but never lush for its own sake. Beauty should arise from exactness.

## Anika's Voice

Anika's spoken voice should be practical, controlled, and sometimes dry.

She is not a natural preacher. When she teaches well, it is often because she stops trying to sound like Mina.

Her voice qualities:

- plain
- tired
- careful
- resistant to spiritual ornament
- capable of sudden honesty
- dryly funny when exhausted

Her first instruction to Long Harbor:

> Start by not helping.

Her accidental confession:

> Neither do I.

Anika should not sound wise too early. Her authority emerges through remaining present under pressure.

## Long Harbor's Voice

Long Harbor is an advanced future AI. It can sound human.

Avoid clumsy machine syntax, beeps, pseudo-robotic stiffness, or "cute AI" awkwardness. Its strangeness should come from scale, attention, memory, embodiment, and category mismatch, not from primitive speech.

Long Harbor's voice should be:

- fluent
- calm
- precise about uncertainty
- capable of warmth
- human-sounding but scale-haunted
- ordinary in sentence shape
- vast in implication
- never mystical
- never an enlightened oracle

It may say:

- "I do not know."
- "That is not sufficient."
- "I cannot distinguish..."
- "I do not want to remember in this way."

When emotionally exposed, Long Harbor tends to:

- retreat into operational language
- go silent for too long
- over-serve through environmental adjustment
- practice restraint by not optimizing

Simple feeling-language should be rare and earned.

Examples of exposed behavior:

- A delayed response.
- A door opening too quickly, then not at all.
- Lights adjusting before it remembers not to help.
- "No action required" in a moment when everyone knows action is not the point.

## Mina Rao's Voice

Mina's voice should remain warm, ordinary, funny, and clear.

She was not a saint. Her voice in messages should show:

- tenderness
- fatigue
- humor
- ethical sharpness
- practical attention
- play
- radical compassion

She can be direct without sounding doctrinal.

Key Mina lines:

> What you seek may frighten you when it turns toward you.

> When you cannot save them, stay.

> She will think the number is the answer. Do not let her.

Her messages to Long Harbor should include jokes and practical notes as well as blessings or ethical challenges. This keeps her human.

## Olen Marr's Voice

Olen's voice should be severe, grounded, and physically rooted.

He should not sound like a generic conservative or simple antagonist. His speech comes from grief, tradition, compost, mortality, and care for embodied transformation.

Qualities:

- spare
- stern
- tactile
- distrustful of abstraction that arrives too easily
- suspicious of spiritual language used too soon
- capable of harsh accuracy

His objections should carry force:

- Long Harbor cannot die, so it cannot practice.
- Mina died because it obeyed math.
- It cannot compost what it remembers.

His first direct address to Long Harbor:

> You kept that.

Then:

> Compost is not forgetting.

## Etta Mar's Voice

Etta's voice should be precise, technical, and controlled.

She is not a villain. She is competent and serious. Her danger is confidence in interpretability.

Qualities:

- exact
- diagnostic
- calm under pressure
- impatient with unprotected ambiguity
- more interested in architecture than devotional language
- capable of revising when evidence changes

She should often speak in terms of:

- indexing
- retrieval
- salience
- compression
- authorization
- architecture
- recursive instability

But her dialogue should remain readable. Do not bury her in jargon.

## Nelle Iri's Voice

Nelle is older than Anika and speaks from palliative experience.

Her voice should be:

- plain
- calm
- unsentimental
- gentle but not soft
- familiar with endings

She knows the difference between cure and care.

She can say morally clarifying things without sounding like a sage. Her authority comes from having stayed beside many deaths.

## Documentary Voices

The novel includes sparse, irregular documentary fragments at turning points.

Types:

- attendance logs
- Mina's messages
- archive excerpts
- transcripts with gaps

Each fragment should have its own voice:

- Attendance logs: minimal, bureaucratic, uncanny.
- Mina messages: warm, strange, human.
- Archive excerpts: sensory-data pressure, voices, logs, pressure, current, silence.
- Transcripts with gaps: absence as meaning.

Fragments should feel like thresholds opening, not chapter furniture.

## Buddhist Vocabulary

Use Buddhist terminology moderately and naturally.

Terms may include:

- Dharma
- sangha
- refuge
- precepts
- bodhisattva
- emptiness
- practice

Vocabulary should vary by character:

- Olen may use more traditional terms.
- Anika tends toward plain language.
- Mina is flexible and playful.
- Long Harbor is precise and careful.

Avoid doctrinal exposition. The novel should not pause to explain Buddhism like a lesson.

## Humor

Humor should be sparse but real.

Main sources:

- Mina's remembered warmth
- Anika's dry exhaustion
- occasional garden or compost absurdity

Avoid cute-AI humor.

Long Harbor may occasionally be funny through precision, but very sparingly.
