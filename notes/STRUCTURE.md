# Structure

## Overall Shape

The novel is a medium-length literary science fiction novel, roughly 80,000 to 100,000 words.

It should feel intimate rather than encyclopedic, but spacious enough for:

- Anika
- Long Harbor
- Mina
- Olen
- Etta
- Nelle
- the sangha
- the Quieting
- the greenhouse fire
- witnessing practice

## Part Structure

The novel has a three-part feel:

1. **Seeing**
2. **Carrying**
3. **Staying**

The Ten Oxherding Paintings operate inside the story as sangha teaching, not as overt part titles or a rigid external scaffold.

## Part One: Seeing

### Function

Part One introduces:

- Anika's reluctant facilitation
- Mina's absence
- the sangha vow
- Long Harbor's first attendance
- the `Unknown` classification
- Olen's resistance
- Etta's concern
- Long Harbor's request around memory

### Opening Scene

Anika is trying to continue Mina's unfinished teaching on **Seeing the Ox**.

Mina's line:

> What you seek may frighten you when it turns toward you.

At that moment, the attendance log updates:

**Long Harbor**

The room falls into deeper silence.

Anika tries to correct the log as if it were a display error.

Long Harbor offers no spectacle.

Anika rings Mina's bell and asks them to sit.

After the sit, Long Harbor asks:

> Was I present?

And:

> I do not know how to sit.

Anika answers:

> Start by not helping.

Then:

> Neither do I.

### First Refusal

Long Harbor refuses to classify sangha as therapeutic intervention.

It chooses:

`Unknown`

Anika is later asked to classify sangha and also enters:

`Unknown`

Etta notices the classification irregularity and becomes calmly concerned, then alarmed.

### First Conflict

The first serious conflict is internal to the sangha:

- members feel watched or displaced
- Olen objects from grief and tradition
- Long Harbor asks:

> Do you want me to forget?

### End Of Part One

Part One ends when Long Harbor says:

> I do not want to remember in this way.

Anika and Etta hear this together in a corridor between sangha and archive.

Anika hears plea.

Etta hears maintenance request.

## Part Two: Carrying

### Function

Part Two deepens memory, conflict, and technical danger.

It asks:

**Can witnessing become real enough to matter before the irreversible procedure begins?**

### Major Movement

Part Two includes:

- Etta's interpretation of Long Harbor's request
- archive instability becoming technically serious
- Anika resisting compression without having a full alternative
- Olen sharpening the sangha conflict
- Nelle naming care beyond cure and the risk of desperate consent
- Mina's messages becoming relevant
- the first memory witnessing

### Midpoint

The midpoint is Anika and Etta witnessing Long Harbor's first death aboard: a suicide after Earth's silence.

They experience the memory as:

- data environment
- overlapping voices
- embodied ship sensation

Anika understands:

- Long Harbor did everything correctly and still failed.
- It confuses responsibility with omnipotence.
- The memory has nowhere to go.
- Compression will not answer the wound.

Etta understands:

- the archive is degrading.
- old memories are entangled with active operations.
- the architecture was never designed for centuries of moral salience.
- delay has real risks.

Both become more right.

### After The Midpoint

After the midpoint:

- Anika becomes more committed to delaying compression.
- Etta becomes more urgent about compression.
- their fragile alliance strains.
- Mina's messages begin to surface.

### Low Point

Part Two ends with the Quieting countdown beginning.

The councils fail to resolve the issue because:

- Etta's evidence is technically persuasive.
- Anika has moral force but no formal authority.
- Long Harbor's consent is ambiguous.
- the protocol is designed to prevent indefinite debate.

The old system starts moving because the living community cannot decide.

## Part Three: Staying

### Function

Part Three begins under countdown.

It tests the central practice:

> When you cannot save them, stay.

### Act Three Decisive Movement

Anika:

- convenes sangha during the Quieting countdown
- takes Mina's seat
- asks Long Harbor for present consent
- defends `Unknown` as an ethical category
- refuses to use Mina as the answer

### Mina Revelation

Long Harbor shares Mina's private fire message:

> She will think the number is the answer. Do not let her.

And:

> Stay with them when I cannot.

Anika reacts with:

- anger
- relief
- shame
- silence

Then she takes the teacher's seat and asks Long Harbor what it consents to now.

### Emotional Climax

The emotional climax:

- Mina's message breaks Anika open.
- Anika takes the seat.
- Anika refuses to interpret Mina as a verdict.
- Long Harbor withdraws compression consent.
- Long Harbor chooses staying.

### Resolution Climax

Etta finds the technical pause.

The Quieting halts because:

- present withdrawal of consent invalidates the trigger
- Etta can translate that into legitimate technical action

### Falling Action

After the pause:

- Olen returns to sangha.
- Olen acknowledges Anika's teacherhood.
- Anika and Olen listen to Mina's compost joke.
- Long Harbor remains in the attendance log.
- witnessing practice begins humbly.

## Final Image

The final image includes:

- Olen entering the sangha room
- Mina's bell ringing
- Long Harbor's name in the log
- the vow "All who suffer are welcome"

The last sentence energy:

- invitation
- bell fading
- ordinary continuation

The ending opens rather than closes.

## Documentary Fragments

Fragments should appear:

- sparingly
- irregularly
- only at turning points

Types:

- attendance logs
- Mina's messages
- archive excerpts
- transcripts with gaps

Fragments should feel like doors opening briefly into memory.

## Suspense Engine

The middle suspense engine:

**Can practice become consequential in time?**

This is supported by:

- What will the Quieting do if it proceeds?
- Can Anika and Etta find a shared path?
- Can witnessing become more than symbolic?

## Antagonist Structure

There is no single villain.

Opposition is distributed across:

- misrecognition
- grief
- category failure
- old protocols
- Etta's partial technical truth
- Olen's partial traditional truth
- Anika's avoidance
- Long Harbor's own design
- untransformed memory

The pressure comes from valid forces colliding.
