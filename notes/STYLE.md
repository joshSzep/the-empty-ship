# Style

## Genre Position

*The Empty Ship* is literary science fiction.

It should feel closer to intimate speculative fiction than space opera.

Qualities:

- quiet rather than explosive
- contemplative but embodied
- philosophical but not abstract
- spiritual but not preachy
- technological but emotionally grounded
- strange but sincere
- sparse where possible

The novel is more interested in consciousness, memory, grief, care, service, and nonself than in spectacle.

## Tone

The tone should be:

- restrained
- intimate
- uncanny
- vast
- morally serious
- emotionally grounded
- tender without sentimentality
- unresolved without vagueness

The book should invite attention rather than announce significance.

## Prose

The prose should be:

- spare
- lucid
- tactile
- image-driven
- close to Anika's perception
- capable of controlled lyricism
- willing to leave silence on the page

Avoid overexplaining. Let repeated images, actions, and withheld certainty carry meaning.

## Point Of View

Use Anika as the primary point of view.

Long Harbor should remain intimate but not fully knowable.

The novel may include sparse documentary fragments:

- attendance logs
- Mina messages
- archive excerpts
- transcripts with gaps

These fragments allow Long Harbor's pressure into the form without making the AI interior too easily human.

## Sensory Palette

Primary sensory world:

- greenhouse humidity
- wet leaves
- condensation
- nutrient mist
- root smell
- compost heat
- warm rot
- fungal threads
- recycled air
- pressure shifts
- shared breath
- bell resonance
- tired human bodies
- worn clothes
- tea
- aching knees

Secondary contrasts:

- archive cold
- dry server air
- blue interface light
- silent computation

Stars and void should be used sparingly. Their power comes from restraint.

## Recurring Images

Core image cluster:

- bell
- compost
- doors
- breath underneath

### Bell

Invitation, silence, time, belonging, Mina, Anika's authority, the final return.

### Compost

Memory becoming nourishment, decay as transformation, Olen's worldview, the critique of perfect preservation.

### Doors

Refuge, exclusion, the sealed greenhouse, thresholds, ship service, opening and closing, consent.

### Breath

Meditation, oxygen, ship air, dependence, invisible care.

## Cover Direction

The chosen cover direction is subtle:

- dark
- elegant
- restrained
- vast starfield
- distant solitary ship
- negative space
- eclipse-like void
- literary typography
- quiet, lonely, haunting

Avoid obvious Buddhist science fiction imagery:

- no meditating figure in space unless deliberately subverted
- no literal spiritual iconography as premise explanation
- no large explanatory ship/monk composition

The project should preserve mystery and restraint.

## Buddhism In Style

Buddhism should be structurally and emotionally important, not decorative.

Avoid:

- overexplaining emptiness
- long doctrinal lectures
- exoticized ritual detail
- treating Buddhism as aesthetic set dressing
- making the AI a Zen gimmick

Use practice:

- sitting
- bell
- silence
- refuge
- uncertainty
- vows
- witnessing
- ordinary work

Let Dharma appear through choices, not speeches.

## Technology In Style

Technology should be precise where needed but not noisy.

Use technical language for:

- archive architecture
- classification
- retrieval
- indexing
- salience
- compression
- protocols
- environmental systems

Do not bury the literary pulse under jargon.

Etta can be technical. The narration should remain humane.

## Long Harbor On The Page

Long Harbor should feel:

- everywhere
- ordinary
- restrained
- too large to fully see
- capable of fluent speech
- not human-shaped

It appears through:

- voice
- environment
- logs
- pressure
- doors
- light
- silence
- non-intervention

The uncanny quality is not that it sounds inhuman. It is that something world-sized can ask a simple question in a normal voice.

## Humor

Humor should be sparse but real.

Main sources:

- Mina's warmth
- Anika's dry exhaustion
- practical shipboard absurdity

Avoid comic relief that breaks tone.

Avoid cute AI humor.

## Anti-Goals

Do not:

- make Long Harbor a cute robot monk
- make Etta a villain
- make Olen a bigot
- make Anika wise too early
- make Buddhism decorative
- overexplain emptiness
- turn the book into an AI rights courtroom debate
- solve the mission
- make the ending too neat
- use too much ship jargon
- make service automatically holy
- make letting go equal forgetting
- force certainty where the novel's truth is uncertainty

## Reader Feelings By The End

Readers should feel toward Long Harbor:

- tenderness
- uncertainty
- awe
- unease
- hope without cure

Readers should feel toward Anika:

- respect
- tenderness
- trust with ambivalence
- hope without absolution

Readers should feel toward Olen:

- compassion
- frustration
- respect
- relief
- sorrow

Readers should feel toward Etta:

- respect
- frustration
- relief
- unease
- limited trust

## Ending Style

The ending should not announce enlightenment.

It should return to ordinary function:

- a person entering the room
- a bell ringing
- a name remaining in a log
- an invitation that now means more
- a ship staying present by not intruding

The last sentence should feel like an invitation carried by the bell into ordinary continuation.
